The Fourth Matter

Dördüncü Mesele

One should not accept bounties which arrive at the hands of apparent causes on account of the causes.

Esbab-ı zahiriye eliyle gelen nimetleri, o esbab hesabına almamak gerektir.

If a cause like an animal or a tree does not possess will, it gives the bounty directly on account of Almighty God.

Eğer o sebep ihtiyar sahibi değilse -mesela, hayvan ve ağaç gibi- doğrudan doğruya Cenab-ı Hak hesabına verir.

It says: “In the Name of God” through the tongue of disposition and gives it to you.

Madem o, lisan-ı hal ile Bismillah der, sana verir.

So you too should say: “In the Name of God,” and take it for God’s sake.

Sen de Allah hesabına olarak Bismillah de, al.

If the cause possesses will, he should say: “In the Name of God,” and you may accept it, otherwise you should refuse it.

Eğer o sebep ihtiyar sahibi ise o Bismillah demeli, sonra ondan al, yoksa alma.

Apart from its explicit meaning, the verse,
Eat not of [meats] on which God’s name has not been pronounced,(6:121) has an implicit meaning:

Çünkü وَلاَ تَاْكُلُوا مِمَّا لَمْ يُذْكَرِ اسْمُ اللّٰهِ عَلَيْهِ âyetinin mana-yı sarîhinden başka bir mana-yı işarîsi şudur ki:

Do not partake of bounties that do not recall the True Bestower of Bounties and are not given in His name.

"Mün'im-i Hakiki'yi hatıra getirmeyen ve onun namıyla verilmeyen nimeti yemeyiniz!" demektir.

Since this is so, both the one who gives and the one who receives should say “In the name of God.”

O halde hem veren Bismillah demeli hem alan Bismillah demeli.

If the giver does not say it and you are in need, you say, “In the Name of God,” and seeing the hand of divine mercy upon him, kiss it in thanks, and take the bounty from him.

Eğer o Bismillah demiyor fakat sen de almaya muhtaç isen sen, Bismillah de, onun başı üstünde rahmet-i İlahiyenin elini gör, şükür ile öp, ondan al.

That is to say, look from the bounty to the bestowal, and from the bestowal think of the True Bestower. To reflect in this way is a sort of thanks.

Yani nimetten in'ama bak, in'amdan Mün'im-i Hakiki'yi düşün. Bu düşünmek bir şükürdür.

Then if you wish, offer a prayer for the apparent means, since it was by his hand that the bounty was sent to you.

Sonra o zahirî vasıtaya istersen dua et.
Çünkü o nimet onun eliyle size gönderildi.

What deceives those who worship apparent causes is the two things coming together or being together, which is called ‘association;’ they suppose the two things cause one another.

Esbab-ı zahiriyeyi perestiş edenleri aldatan; iki şeyin beraber gelmesi veya bulunmasıdır ki "iktiran" tabir edilir, birbirine illet zannetmeleridir.

Also, since the non-existence of one thing is the cause of a bounty’s non-existence, they suppose that the thing’s existence is also the cause of the bounty’s existence.

Hem bir şeyin ademi, bir nimetin ma'dum olmasına illet olduğundan, tevehhüm eder ki: O şeyin vücudu dahi o nimetin vücuduna illettir.

They offer their thanks and gratitude to the thing and fall into error.

Şükrünü, minnettarlığını o şeye verir, hataya düşer.

For a bounty’s existence results from all the bounty’s conditions and preliminaries.

Çünkü bir nimetin vücudu, o nimetin umum mukaddimatına ve şeraitine terettüp eder.

Whereas the bounty’s non-existence occurs through the non-existence of only a single condition.

Halbuki o nimetin ademi, bir tek şartın ademiyle oluyor.

For example, someone who does not open the canal to water the garden is the reason and cause of the garden drying up and the non-existence of bounties.

Mesela, bir bahçeyi sulayan cetvelin deliğini açmayan adam, o bahçenin kurumasına ve o nimetlerin ademine sebep ve illet oluyor.

But the existence of the garden’s bounties is dependent on hundreds of conditions besides the man’s duty

and the bounties come into being through dominical will and power, which are the true cause.

illet-i hakiki olan kudret ve irade-i Rabbaniye ile vücuda gelir.

So understand just how clear is the error of this sophistry and how mistaken are those who worship causes!

Yes, ‘association’ is one thing and the cause is another. You receive a bounty, but the intention of a person to bestow it on you was the ‘associate’ of the bounty, not the cause.

Evet iktiran ayrıdır, illet ayrıdır. Bir nimet sana geliyor fakat bir insanın sana karşı ihsan niyeti, o nimete mukarin olmuş fakat illet olmamış.

The Fifth Matter

Beşinci Mesele

Just as if the property of a community is given to one man, it is wrong; or if one man lays hands on charitable foundations which belong to the community, he does wrong;

Nasıl ki bir cemaatin malı bir adama verilse zulüm olur. Veya cemaate ait vakıfları bir adam zapt etse zulmeder.

so too to ascribe to the leader or master of a community the results of that community’s labours or the honour and merits resulting from its good works, is wrong both for the communit y and for the leader or master.

Öyle de cemaatin sa'yleriyle hasıl olan bir neticeyi veya cemaatin haseneleriyle terettüp eden bir şerefi, bir fazileti, o cemaatin reisine veya üstadına vermek hem cemaate hem de o üstad veya reise zulümdür.

Because to do so flatters his egotism and encourages pride. While being the door-keeper, he supposes himself to be the king.

Çünkü enaniyeti okşar, gurura sevk eder. Kendini kapıcı iken, padişah zannettirir.

He also does wrong to himself. Indeed, he opens the way to a sort of concealed association of partners with God.

Hem kendi nefsine de zulmeder. Belki bir nevi şirk-i hafîye yol açar.

Yes, the colonel cannot claim for himself the booty, victory, and glory belonging to a regiment which conquers a citadel.

Evet bir kaleyi fetheden bir taburun ganimetini ve muzafferiyet ve şerefini, binbaşısı alamaz.

The master and spiritual guide should not be considered to be the source and origin,

Evet üstad üstad ve mürşid, masdar ve menba telakki edilmemek gerektir.

but known to be the place of reflection and manifestation.

Belki mazhar ve ma'kes olduklarını bilmek lâzımdır.

For example, heat and light reach you by means of a mirror.

Mesela, hararet ve ziya, sana bir âyine vasıtasıyla gelir.

It would be crazy if you forget the sun, and considering the mirror to be the source, are grateful to it instead of being grateful to the sun.

Senden güneşe karşı minnettar olmaya bedel, âyineyi masdar telakki edip, güneşi unutup ona minnettar olmak divaneliktir.

The mirror should be preserved because it is the place of manifestation.

Thus, the guide’s spirit and heart are a mirror; they are the place for reflecting the effulgence emanating from Almighty God. He is the means of its being reflected to his followers.

İşte mürşidin ruhu ve kalbi bir âyinedir. Cenab-ı Hak'tan gelen feyze ma'kes olur, müridine aksedilmesine de vesile olur.

He should not be ascribed a station higher with regard to the effulgence than that of being the means.

It sometimes even happens that a master considered to be the source is neither the place of manifestation nor the source.

The follower supposes the effulgences he receives due to the purity of his sincerity, or his strength of attachment, or his concentration on his master, or in other ways, to have come from the mirror of his master’s spirit.

Belki müridinin safvet-i ihlasıyla ve kuvvet-i irtibatıyla ve ona hasr-ı nazar ile o mürid başka yolda aldığı füyuzatı, üstadının mir'at-ı ruhundan gelmiş görüyor.

Like by means of mesmerism, some people open up a window onto the World of Similitudes by gazing attentively at a mirror, and observe strange and wonderful things in the mirror.

Nasıl ki bazı adam, manyetizma vasıtasıyla bir cama dikkat ede ede âlem-i misale karşı hayalinde bir pencere açılır. O âyinede çok garaibi müşahede eder.

Bazı eblehler var ki güneşi tanımadıkları için bir âyinede güneşi görse âyineyi sevmeye başlar.

Third Sign

Üçüncü Remiz

O man! The strangest state the All-Wise Creator has included in your nature is your inability to settle in the whole world;

Ey insan! Fâtır-ı Hakîm'in senin mahiyetine koyduğu en garib bir halet şudur ki: Bazen dünyaya yerleşemiyorsun.

like someone suffocating in prison, you gasp for somewhere wider than the world. Yet you enter the minutest matter, a memory, a moment, and settle in it.

The Nineteenth Flash

Ondokuzuncu Lem'a

[On Frugality]

[İktisad Risalesi]

(This treatise is about frugality and contentment, and wastefulness and extravagance.)

(İktisad ve kanaate; israf ve tebzîre dairdir.)

In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate. Eat and drink, but waste not by excess.(7:31)

بِسْمِ اللّهِ الرّحْمنِ الرّحِيمِ

كُلُوا وَ اشْرَبُوا وَ لاَ تُسْرِفُوا

This verse gives most important and wise instruction in the form of categorically commanding frugality and clearly prohibiting wastefulness.

Şu âyet-i kerîme, iktisada kat'î emir ve israftan nehy-i sarih suretinde gâyet mühim bir ders-i hikmet veriyor.

The matter contains seven Points.

Şu mes'elede "Yedi Nükte" var.

First Point

Birinci Nükte

The All-Compassionate Creator desires thanks in return for the bounties He bestows on mankind,

Hâlık-ı Rahîm, nev-i beşere verdiği nîmetlerin mukabilinde şükür istiyor.

while wastefulness is contrary to thanks,

İsraf ise; şükre zıttır,

and slights the bounty and causes loss.

nîmete karşı hasâretli bir istihfaftır.

Frugality, however, shows respect for the bounty and is profitable.

İktisad ise, nîmete karşı ticaretli bir ihtiramdır.

Yes, frugality is both a sort of thanks,

Evet iktisad hem bir şükr-ü mânevî,

and shows respect towards the divine mercy manifested in the bounties,

hem nimetlerdeki Rahmet-i İlâhiyyeye karşı bir hürmet,

and most definitely is the cause of plenty.

hem kat'î bir surette sebeb-i bereket,

So too, like abstinence, it is health-giving for the body,

hem bedene perhiz gibi bir medâr-ı sıhhat,

and since it saves a person from the degradation of what is in effect begging, is a cause of self-respect.

hem mânevî dilencilik zilletinden kurtaracak bir sebeb-i izzet,

It is also a powerful means of experiencing the pleasure to be found in bounties, and tasting that pleasure in bounties which apparently afford no pleasure.

hem nimet içindeki lezzeti hissetmesine ve zâhiren lezzetsiz görünen nîmetlerdeki lezzeti tatmasına kuvvetli bir sebebdir.

As for wastefulness, since it is opposed to these instances of wisdom, it has grave consequences.

İsraf ise, mezkûr hikmetlere muhâlif olduğundan, vahim neticeleri vardır.

Second Point

İkinci Nükte

The All-Wise Maker created the human body in the form of a wonderful palace and resembling a well-ordered city.

Fâtır-ı Hakîm, insanın vücudunu mükemmel bir saray suretinde ve muntazam bir şehir misalinde yaratmış.

The sense of taste in the mouth is like a door- keeper, and the nerves and blood vessels like telephone and telegraph wires;

Ağızdaki kuvve-i zâikayı bir kapıcı, â'sab ve damarları telefon ve telgraf telleri gibi
#190

they are the means by which the sense of taste communicates with the stomach, which is at the centre of the body, and informs it of the food that enters the mouth.

kuvve-i zâika ile, merkez-i vücuddaki mîde ile bir medâr-ı muhabereleridir ki, ağıza gelen maddeyi o damarlarla haber verir.

If the body and stomach have no use for it, it says: “Forbidden!”, and expels it.

Bedene, mîdeye lüzumu yoksa "Yasaktır!" der, dışarı atar.

And sometimes the food is harmful and bitter as well as not being beneficial for the body, and it spits it out immediately.

Bazen da bedene menfaatı olmamakla beraber zararlı ve acı ise; hemen dışarı atar, yüzüne tükürür.

Thus, since the sense of taste is a doorkeeper, from the point of view of administering the body, the stomach is a master and a ruler.

İşte mâdem ağızdaki kuvve-i zâika bir kapıcıdır; mîde, cesedin idaresi noktasında bir efendi ve bir hâkimdir.

If the gifts arriving at the palace or city and those given to the palace’s ruler are worth one hundred liras,

O saraya veyahût o şehre gelen ve sarayın hâkimine verilen hediyenin yüz derece kıymeti varsa,

only five liras’ worth is appropriate for the doorkeeper in the form of a tip,

kapıcıya bahşiş nev'inden ancak beş derecesi muvafık olur, fazla olamaz.

lest he becomes conceited and is corrupted, then forgetting his duty he lets revolutionaries into the palace who will give him a bigger tip.

Tâ ki; kapıcı gururlanıp, baştan çıkıp vazifeyi unutup, fazla bahşiş veren ihtilâlcileri saray dahiline sokmasın.

In consequence of this mystery we shall now imagine two mouthfuls.

İşte bu sırra binâen, şimdi iki lokma farzediyoruz.

One consists of nutritious food like cheese and egg and costs forty para,* and the other is of the choicest pastries and costs ten kurush.

Bir lokma, peynir ve yumurta gibi mugaddi maddeden kırk para; diğer lokma, en âlâ baklavadan on kuruş olsa..

Before entering the mouth, there is no difference in these two mouthfuls with respect to the body, they are equal.

bu iki lokma, ağıza girmeden, beden itibariyle farkları yoktur, müsâvidirler;

*{There were forty para to a kurush, and a hundred kurush to a lira.(Tr.)}

And after passing down the throat, they are still equal in nourishing the body.

boğazdan geçtikten sonra, cesed beslemesinde yine müsâvidirler.

Indeed, forty paras’ worth of cheese is sometimes more nutritious.

Belki, bazen kırk paralık peynir daha iyi besler.

Only, in regard to pampering the sense of taste in the mouth, there is a half-minute difference.

Yalnız, ağızdaki kuvve-i zâikayı okşamak noktasında yarım dakika bir fark var.

You can see from this what a meaningless and harmful waste it is to increase the cost from forty para to ten kurush for the sake of half a minute.

Yarım dakika hatırı için kırk paradan on kuruşa çıkmak, ne kadar mânâsız ve zararlı bir israf olduğu kıyas edilsin.

Now, although the gift arriving for the palace’s ruler is worth one lira, to give the doorkeeper a tip nine times bigger than his due will corrupt him.

Şimdi, saray hâkimine gelen hediye kırk para olmakla beraber, kapıcıya dokuz defa fazla bahşiş vermek, kapıcıyı baştan çıkarır,

He will declare: “I am the ruler,” and will allow to enter whoever gives him the biggest tip and most pleasure; he will cause a revolution and conflagration to break out.

"Hâkim benim" der. Kim fazla bahşiş ve lezzet verse onu içeriye sokacak, ihtilâl verecek, yangınçıkaracak,

Then he will compel them to cry out: “Oh! Call the doctor and get him to put out this fire in my stomach and bring down my temperature!”

"Aman doktor gelsin, hararetimi teskin etsin, ateşimi söndürsün." dedirmeye mecbur edecek.

Thus, frugality and contentment are in conformity with divine wisdom;

İşte iktisat ve kanaat, hikmet-i İlâhiyyeye tevfik-ı harekettir.

they treat the sense of taste as a doorkeeper and give it its remuneration accordingly.

Kuvve-i zâikayı kapıcı hükmünde tutup, ona göre bahşiş verir.

As for wastefulness, since it is to act contrarily to wisdom, it swiftly receives its punishment, upsets the stomach, and causes real appetite to be lost.

İsraf ise; o hikmete zıt hareket ettiği için çabuk tokat yer, mîdeyi karıştırır, iştiha-yı hakîkîyi kaybeder.

Producing from the unnecessary variety of foods a false and artificial appetite, it causes indigestion and illness.

Tenevvü-ü et'imeden gelen sun'î bir iştiha-yı kâzibe ile yedirir, hazımsızlığa sebebiyet verir, hasta eder.
