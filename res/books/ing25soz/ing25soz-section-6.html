Ninth Point of Eloquence

Dokuzuncu Nükte-i Belâgat

It sometimes happens that the All-Wise Qur'an mentions certain particular aims,

Kur'an-ı Hakîm kâh olur cüz'î bazı maksadları zikreder.

then in order to impel the mind by means of them,

Sonra o cüz'iyat vasıtasıyla küllî makamlara zihinleri sevketmek için,

confirms, establishes, verifies, and proves the aims through the Divine Names, which are like universal rules.

o cüz'î maksadı, bir kaide-i külliye hükmünde olan esma-i hüsna ile takrir ederek tesbit eder, tahkik edip isbat eder. Meselâ:

For example:
God has indeed heard the statement of the woman who pleads with you concerning her husband and carries her complaint to God; and God [always] hears the arguments between both sides among you; for God is All-Hearing, All-Seeing. {Qur'an, 58:1.}

قَدْ سَمِعَ اللّٰهُ قَوْلَ الَّتِى تُجَادِلُكَ فِى زَوْجِهَا وَتَشْتَكِى اِلَى اللّٰهِ وَاللّٰهُ يَسْمَعُ تَحَاوُرَكُمَا اِنَّ اللّهَ سَمِيعٌ بَصِيرٌ

Here the Qur'an is saying: "Almighty God is absolutely All-Hearing; He hears everything,

İşte Kur'an der: "Cenab-ı Hak, Semi'-i Mutlak'tır, her şeyi işitir.

even, through the Divine Name of Truth, a wife arguing with you and complaining about her husband, a truly insignificant matter.

Hattâ en cüz'î bir macera olan ve zevcinden teşekki eden bir zevcenin sana karşı mücadelesini Hak ismiyle işitir.

And since women manifest the subtlest manifestations of mercy and are mines of self-sacrificing compassion, He hears through the Name of Most Compassionate the rightful claim of a woman and her complaint to Him, and through the Name of Truth takes it seriously, affording it the greatest importance."

Hem rahmetin en latif cilvesine mazhar ve şefkatın en fedakâr bir hakikatına maden olan bir kadının haklı olarak zevcinden davasını ve Cenab-ı Hakk'a şekvasını umûr-u azîme suretinde Rahîm ismiyle ehemmiyetle işitir ve Hak ismiyle ciddiyetle bakar."

Thus, in order to make this particular aim universal,

İşte bu cüz'î maksadı küllîleştirmek için,

One outside the sphere of contingency of the universe Who hears and sees a minor incident among creatures, must of necessity hear and see all things,

mahlukatın en cüz'î bir hâdisesini işiten, gören; kâinatın daire-i imkânîsinden hariç bir zât, elbette her şeyi işitir, her şeyi görür bir zât olmak lâzımgelir.

and One Who is Sustainer of the universe of necessity sees the suffering of insignificant creatures within the universe who are wronged, and hears their cries.

Ve kâinata Rab olan, kâinat içinde mazlum küçük mahlukların dertlerini görmek, feryatlarını işitmek gerektir.

One who does not see their suffering and does not hear their cries for help cannot be the Sustainer.

Dertlerini görmeyen, feryatlarını işitmeyen, "Rab" olamaz.

In which case, it establishes two mighty truths with the phrase,
For God is All-Hearing, All-Seeing.

Öyle ise, اِنَّ اللّهَ سَمِيعٌ بَصِيرٌ cümlesiyle iki hakikat-ı azîmeyi tesbit eder.

And, for example: Glory be to [God] who did take His servant by night from the Sacred Mosque to the Farthest Mosque, whose precincts We did bless, in order that We might show him some of Our signs, for He is indeed All-Hearing, All-Seeing. {Qur'an, 17:1.}

Hem meselâ:
سُبْحَانَ الَّذِى اَسْرَى بِعَبْدِهِ لَيْلاً مِنَ الْمَسْجِدِ الْحَرَامِ اِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ اْلاَقْصَى الَّذِى بَارَكْنَا حَوْلَهُ لِنُرِيَهُ مِنْ آيَاتِنَا اِنَّهُ هُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ

Here, after mentioning the Noble Messenger's (Peace and blessings be upon him) journey from the Sacred Mosque in Mecca to the Farthest Mosque in Jerusalem, which was the start of his Ascension, the Qur'an says: for He is indeed All-Hearing, All-Seeing.

İşte Kur'an, Resul-i Ekrem Aleyhissalâtü Vesselâm'ın mi'racının mebdei olan, Mescid-i Haram'dan Mescid-i Aksa'ya olan seyeranını zikrettikten sonra اِنَّهُ هُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ der.

The pronoun He refers to either Almighty God or to the Prophet.

Tenth Point of Eloquence

Onuncu Nükte-i Belâgat

It sometimes happens that a verse mentions man's rebellious acts, then restrains him with severe threats.

Kâh oluyor âyet, insanın isyankârane amellerini zikreder, şedid bir tehdid ile zecreder.

Then, so that the severity of the threats should not cast him into despair and hopelessness, it concludes with some Divine Names which point to His mercy and console him.

Sonra şiddet-i tehdid, ye'se ve ümidsizliğe atmamak için, rahmetine işaret eden bir kısım esma ile hâtime verir, teselli eder.

For example:
Say: if there had been [other] gods with Him -as they say- behold they would certainly have sought out a way to the Lord of the Throne! * Glory be to Him!
