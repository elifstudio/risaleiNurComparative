#31

FOURTEENTH REMEDY

ONDÖRDÜNCÜ DEVA

O sick person whose eyes have lost their sight!

Ey gözüne perde gelen hasta!

If you knew what a light and spiritual eye is to be found beneath the cataract that may cover a believer’s eyes, you would exclaim: “A hundred thousand thanks to my Compassionate Sustainer.”

Eğer ehl-i imanın gözüne gelen perdenin altında nasıl bir nur ve manevî bir göz olduğunu bilsen “Yüzbin şükür Rabb-ı Rahîmime” dersin.

I shall recount an incident to you to explain this salve. It is as follows:

Bu merhemi izah için bir hâdise söyleyeceğim. Şöyle ki:

One time, the aunt of Süleyman from Barla, who served me for eight years with total loyalty and willingness, became blind.

Bana sekiz sene kemal-i sadakatla hiç gücendirmeden hizmet eden Barla’lı Süleyman’ın halasının, bir vakit gözü kapandı.

Thinking well of me a hundred times more than was my due, the righteous woman caught me by the door of the mosque and asked me to pray for her sight to be restored.

O sâliha kadın, bana karşı haddimden yüz derece fazla hüsn-ü zan ederek, “Gözümün açılması için dua et” diyerek, câmi kapısında beni yakaladı.

So I made the blessed woman’s righteousness the intercessor for my supplication, and beseeching Almighty God, I prayed: “O Lord! Restore her sight out of respect for her righteousness.”

Ben de, o mübarek ve meczube kadının salahatını duama şefaatçı yapıp, “Ya Rabbi, onun salahatı hürmetine onun gözünü aç” diye yalvardım.

Two days later, an oculist from Burdur came and removed the cataract. Forty days later she again lost her sight.

İkinci gün Burdur’lu bir göz hekimi geldi, gözünü açtı. Kırk gün sonra yine gözü kapandı.

I was most upset and prayed fervently for her. God willing, the prayer was accepted for her life in the Hereafter,

Ben çok müteessir oldum, çok dua ettim. İnşâallah o dua, âhireti için kabul olmuştur.

otherwise that prayer of mine would have been a most mistaken malediction for her.

Yoksa benim o duam, onun hakkında gayet yanlış bir beddua olurdu.

For forty days had remained till her death; forty days later she had died -May God have mercy on her.

Çünki eceli kırk gün kalmıştı. Kırk gün sonra -Allah rahmet etsin- vefat eyledi.

Thus, in place of the woman looking sorrowfully at the gardens of Barla with the eye of old age, she profited by in her grave being able to gaze for forty thousand days on the gardens of Paradise.

İşte o merhume, kırk gün Barla’nın hazînane bağlarına rikkatli ihtiyarlık gözüyle bakmasına bedel; kabrinde, Cennet bağlarını kırkbin günlerde seyredeceğini kazandı.

For her belief was strong and she was completely righteous.

Çünki imanı kuvvetli, salahatı şiddetli idi.

#33

Yes, if a believer loses his sight and enters the grave blind, in accordance with his degree he may gaze on the world of light to a much greater extent than others in their graves.

Evet bir mü’min gözüne perde çekilse ve gözü kapalı kabre girse, derecesine göre, ehl-i kuburdan çok ziyade o âlem-i nuru temaşa edebilir.

Just as we see many things in this world that blind believers do not see,

Bu dünyada nasıl çok şeyleri biz görüyoruz, kör olan mü’minler görmüyorlar.

if they depart with belief, those blind people see to a greater extent than other dead in their graves.

Kabirde o körler, iman ile gitmiş ise, o derece ehl-i kuburdan ziyade görür.

As though looking through the most powerful telescopes, they can see and gaze on the gardens of Paradise like the cinema, in accordance with their degree.

En uzak gösteren dûrbînlerle bakar nev’inde, kabrinde derecesine göre Cennet bağlarını sinema gibi görüp temaşa ederler.

Thus, with thanks and patience you can find beneath the veil on your present eye an eye which is thus light-filled, and with which while beneath the earth you can see and observe Paradise above the skies.

İşte böyle gayet nurlu ve toprak altında iken göklerin üstündeki Cennet’i görecek ve seyredecek bir gözü, bu gözündeki perde altında şükür ile sabır ile bulabilirsin.

That which will raise the veil from your eye, the eye doctor that will allow you to look with that eye, is the All-Wise Qur’an.

İşte o perdeyi senin gözünden kaldıracak, o gözle seni baktıracak göz hekimi, Kur’an-ı Hakîm’dir.

#34

FIFTEENTH REMEDY

ONBEŞİNCİ DEVA

O sick person who sighs and laments! Do not look at the outward aspect of illness and sigh, look at its meaning and be pleased.

Ey âh ü enin eden hasta! Hastalığın suretine bakıp âh! eyleme. Manasına bak oh! de.

If the meaning of illness had not been good, the All-Compassionate Creator would not have given illness to the servants He loves most.

Eğer hastalığın manası güzel birşey olmasa idi, Hâlık-ı Rahîm en sevdiği ibadına hastalıkları vermezdi.

Whereas, there is a Hadith the meaning of which is, “Those afflicted with the severest trials are the prophets, then the saints and those like them .”

Yes, there are some illnesses which if they lead to death, are like a sort of martyrdom; they result in a degree of sainthood like martyrdom.

For example, those who die from the illnesses accompanying childbirth

So also there are many blessed illnesses which gain the degree of sainthood for those who die from them.

Moreover, since illness lessens love of the world and attachment to it, it lightens parting from the world through death, which for the worldly is extremely grievous and painful, and it sometimes even makes it desirable.

Hem hastalık, dünya aşkını ve alâkasını hafifleştirdiğinden, vefat ile dünyadan, ehl-i dünya için gayet elîm ve acı olan müfarakatı tahfif eder; bazan da sevdirir.

SIXTEENTH REMEDY

ONALTINCI DEVA

O sick person who complains of his distress!

Ey sıkıntıdan şekva eden hasta!

Illness prompts respect and compassion, which are most important and good in human social life.

Hastalık, hayat-ı içtimaiye-i insaniyede en mühim ve gayet güzel olan hürmet ve merhameti telkin eder.

For it saves man from self-sufficiency, which drives him to unsociableness and unkindness.

Çünki insanı vahşete ve merhametsizliğe sevkeden istiğnadan kurtarıyor.

For according to the meaning of, Indeed man transgresses all bounds * In that he looks upon himself as self-sufficient ,

13 Qur’an, 96:6-7.

Çünki اِنَّ اْلاِنْسَانَ لَيَطْغَى اَنْ رَآهُ اسْتَغْنَى sırrıyla,

an evil-commanding soul which feels self-sufficient due to good health and well-being, does not feel respect towards his brothers in many instances, who are deserving of it.

sıhhat ve âfiyetten gelen istiğnada bulunan bir nefs-i emmare, şâyan-ı hürmet çok uhuvvetlere karşı hürmeti hissetmez.

And he does not feel compassion towards the sick and those smitten by disaster, although they deserve kindness and pity.

Ve şâyan-ı merhamet ve şefkat olan musibetzedelere ve hastalıklılara merhameti duymaz.

Whenever he is ill, he understands his own impotence and want, and he has respect towards his brothers who are worthy of it.

Ne vakit hasta olsa, o hastalıkta aczini ve fakrını anlar, lâyık-ı hürmet olan ihvanlarına ihtiram eder.

He feels respect towards his believing brothers who visit him or assist him.

Ziyaretine gelen veya ona yardım eden mü’min kardeşlerine karşı hürmeti hisseder.

And he feels human kindness, which arises from fellow-feeling, and compassion for those struck by disaster -a most important Islamic characteristic.

Ve rikkat-ı cinsiyeden gelen şefkat-ı insaniye ve en mühim bir haslet-i İslâmiye olan musibetzedelere karşı merhameti hissedip,

And comparing them to himself, he pities them in the true meaning of the word and feels compassion for them.

He does what he can to help them, and at the very least prays for them and goes to visit them to ask them how they are, which is Sunna according to the Shari’a, and thus earns reward.

#38

SEVENTEENTH REMEDY

ONYEDİNCİ DEVA

O sick person who complains at not being able to perform good works due to illness!

Ey hastalık vasıtasıyla hayrat yapamamaktan şekva eden hasta!

Offer thanks! It is illness that opens to you the door of the most sincere of good works.

Şükret, hayratın en hâlisinin kapısını sana açan, hastalıktır.

In addition to continuously gaining reward for the sick person and for those who look after him for God’s sake, illness is a most important means for supplications being accepted.

Hastalık mütemadiyen hastaya ve Lillah için hastaya bakıcılara sevab kazandırmakla beraber, duanın makbuliyetine en mühim bir vesiledir.

Indeed, there is significant reward for believers for looking after the sick.

Evet hastalara bakmak ehl-i iman için mühim sevabı vardır.

Enquiring after their health and visiting the sick -on condition it does not tax them- is Sunna and also atonement for sins.

14 al-Munawi, Fayzu’l-Qadir ii, 45 no: 1285.

There is a Hadith which says, “Receive the prayers of the sick, for their prayers are acceptable.”

15 Ibn Maja, Jana’iz 1;

Especially if the sick are relations, and parents in particular, to look after them is important worship, yielding significant reward.

Bahusus hasta, akrabadan olsa, hususan peder ve vâlide olsa, onlara hizmet mühim bir ibadettir, mühim bir sevabdır.

To please a sick person’s heart and console him, is like significant alms-giving.

Hastaların kalbini hoşnud etmek, teselli vermek, mühim bir sadaka hükmüne geçer.

Fortunate is the person who pleases the easily touched hearts of father and mother at the time of illness, and receives their prayer.

Bahtiyardır o evlâd ki; peder ve vâlidesinin hastalık zamanında, onların seri-üt teessür olan kalblerini memnun edip hayır dualarını alır.

Indeed, even the angels applaud saying: “Ma’shallah! Barekallah!” before loyal scenes of those good offspring who respond at the time of their illness to the compassion of their parents -those most worthy of respect in the life of society- with perfect respect and filial kindness, showing the exaltedness of humanity.

Evet hayat-ı içtimaiyede en muhterem bir hakikat olan peder ve vâlidesinin şefkatlerine mukabil, hastalıkları zamanında kemal-i hürmet ve şefkat-i ferzendane ile mukabele eden o iyi evlâdın vaziyetini ve insaniyetin ulviyetini gösteren o vefadar levhaya karşı, hattâ melaikeler dahi “Mâşâallah, Bârekâllah” deyip alkışlıyorlar.

Yes, there are pleasures at the time of illness which arise from the kindness, pity, and compassion of those around them, and are most pleasant and agreeable and reduce the pains of illness to nothing.

Evet hastalık zamanında, hastalık elemini hiçe indirecek gayet hoş ve ferahlı, etrafında tezahür eden şefkatlerden ve acımak ve merhametlerden gelen lezzetler var.

#40

The acceptability of the prayers of the sick is an important matter.

Hastanın duasının makbuliyeti, ehemmiyetli bir mes’eledir.

For the past thirty or forty years, I myself have prayed to be cured from the illness of lumbago from which I suffer.

Ben otuz-kırk seneden beri, bendeki kulunç denilen bir hastalıktan şifa için dua ederdim.

However, I understood that the illness had been given for prayer.

Ben anladım ki, hastalık dua için verilmiş.

Since through prayer, prayer cannot be removed, that is, since prayer cannot remove itself, I understood that the results of prayer pertain to the Hereafter,

Dua ile duayı, yani dua kendi kendini kaldırmadığından anladım ki, duanın neticesi uhrevîdir;
