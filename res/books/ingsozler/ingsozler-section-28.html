#281

The Second Station of the Twenty-First Word

Yirmibirinci Söz'ün İkinci Makamı

[This comprises five cures for five of the heart's wounds.]

[Kalbin beş yarasına beş merhemi tazammun eder.]

In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate.

بِسْمِ اللّٰهِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

And say: "O my Sustainer! I seek refuge with You from the suggestions of the evil ones * And I seek refuge with you, O my Sustainer, lest they should come near me."

رَبِّ اَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ هَمَزَاتِ الشَّيَاطِينِ ٭ وَاَعُوذُ بِكَ رَبِّ اَنْ يَحْضُرُونِ

O one afflicted with the sickness of scruples! Do you know what your scruples resemble? A calamity! The more importance they are given, the more they grow. If you give them no importance, they die away.

Ey maraz-ı vesvese ile mübtela! Biliyor musun vesvesen neye benzer? Musibete benzer. Ehemmiyet verdikçe şişer, ehemmiyet vermezsen söner.

If you see them as big, they grow bigger. If you see them as small, they grow smaller.

Ona büyük nazarıyla baksan büyür. Küçük görsen, küçülür.

If you fear them, they swell and make you ill.

If you do not fear them, they are light and remain hidden.

Korksan ağırlaşır, hasta eder.

Havf etmezsen hafif olur, mahfî kalır.

If you do not know their true nature, they persist and become established. While if you do know them and recognize them, they disappear.

Mahiyetini bilmezsen devam eder, yerleşir.

Mahiyetini bilsen, onu tanısan gider.

And so, I shall explain only five 'Aspects' which, of the many sorts of these calamitous scruples, are those which most frequently occur.

Öyle ise, şu musibetli vesvesenin aksam-ı kesîresinden kesîr-ül vuku olan yalnız beş vechini beyan edeceğim.

Perhaps it may be curative for you and for me, for these scruples are such that ignorance invites them and knowledge repulses them.

Belki sana ve bana şifa olur. Zira şu vesvese öyle bir şeydir ki, cehil onu davet eder, ilim onu tardeder.

If you do not recognize them they come, if you do recognize them they go.

Tanımazsan gelir, tanısan gider.

FIRST ASPECT - FIRST WOUND

Birinci Vecih - Birinci Yara:

Satan first casts a doubt into the heart. If the heart does not accept it, it turns from a doubt into abuse. It depicts before the imagination some unclean memories and unmannerly, ugly states which resemble abuse,

Şeytan evvelâ şübheyi kalbe atar. Eğer kalb kabul etmezse, şübheden şetme döner. Hayale karşı şetme benzer bazı pis hatıraları ve münafî-i edeb çirkin halleri tasvir eder.

and causes the heart to declare: "Alas!", and fall into despair. The person suffering from scruples supposes that he has acted wrongfully before his Sustainer

Kalbe "Eyvah" dedirtir, ye'se düşürtür. Vesveseli adam zanneder ki; kalbi, Rabbine karşı sû'-i edebde bulunuyor.

and feels a terrible agitation and anxiety. In order to be saved from it, he flees from the Divine presence and wants to plunge into heedlessness. The cure for this wound is this:

Müdhiş bir halecan ve heyecan hisseder. Bundan kurtulmak için huzurdan kaçar, gaflete dalmak ister. Bu yaranın merhemi budur:

O wretched man suffering from scruples! Do not be alarmed! For what comes to your mind is not abuse, but something imaginary. And like to imagine unbelief is not unbelief, to imagine abuse is not abuse either.

Bak ey bîçare vesveseli adam! Telaş etme. Çünki senin hatırına gelen şetm değil, belki tahayyüldür. Tahayyül-ü küfür, küfür olmadığı gibi; tahayyül-ü şetm dahi, şetm değildir.

#282

For according to logic, an imagining is not a judgement, and abuse is a judgement.

Zira mantıkça tahayyül, hüküm değildir. Şetm ise, hükümdür.

Moreover, those ugly words are not the words of your heart, because your heart is saddened and sorry at them.

Hem bununla beraber o çirkin sözler, senin kalbinin sözleri değil. Çünki senin kalbin ondan müteessir ve müteessiftir.

Rather they come from the inner faculty situated near the heart which is a means of Satanic whisperings. The harm of scruples is imagining the harm.

Belki kalbe yakın olan lümme-i şeytanîden geliyor. Vesvesenin zararı, tevehhüm-ü zarardır.

That is, it is to suffer harm in the heart through imagining them to be harmful. For it is imagining to be reality an imagining which is devoid of judgement.

Yani onu zararlı tevehhüm etmekle, kalben mutazarrır olmaktır. Çünki hükümsüz bir tahayyülü hakikat tevehhüm eder.

Also, it is to attribute to the heart Satan's works; to suppose his words to be from it. Such a person thinks it is harmful, so it becomes harmful. That is anyway what Satan wanted.

Hem şeytanın işini kendi kalbine mal eder. Onun sözünü, ondan zanneder. Zarar anlar, zarara düşer. Zâten şeytanın da istediği odur.

SECOND ASPECT

İkinci Vecih

It is this: when meanings arise in the heart, they enter the imagination stripped of form; it is there that they are clothed in an image or form.

budur ki: Manalar kalbden çıktıkları vakit, suretlerden çıplak olarak hayale girerler; oradan suretleri giyerler.

The imagination, always affected by some cause, weaves images of a sort. It leaves on the way the images of the things to which it gives importance.

Hayal ise, her vakit bir sebeb tahtında bir nevi suretleri nesceder. Ehemmiyet verdiği şeyin suretlerini yol üstünde bırakır.

Whatever meaning passes through it, it either clothes it, or wears it, or taints it, or veils it.

Hangi mana geçse ya ona giydirir, ya takar, ya bulaştırır, ya perde eder.

If the meanings are pure and clean, and the images, dirty and base, there is no clothing, but there is contact.

Eğer manalar münezzeh ve temiz iseler, suretler mülevves ve rezil ise giymek yoktur, fakat temas var.

The man with scruples confuses the contact with being clothed. He exclaims: "Alas! How corrupted my heart has become. This lowness has made me despicable!" Satan takes advantage of this vein of his. The cure for such a wound is as follows:

Vesveseli adam, teması telebbüsle iltibas eder. "Eyvah!" der. "Kalbim ne kadar bozulmuş. Bu sefillik, bu hısset-i nefs, beni matrud eder." Şeytan onun şu damarından çok istifade eder. Şu yaranın merhemi şudur:

Listen, O you unfortunate! Just as outward cleanliness, which is the means to the correct conduct of your prayers, is not affected by the uncleanness of the inside of your inner organs, and is not spoiled by it,

Dinle ey bîçare! Nasılki, senin namazın edeb-i nezihanesinin vesilesi olan zahirî taharete, batnının bâtınındaki necaset ona tesir etmez ve bozmaz.

so the sacred meanings being close to unclean forms does not harm them.

Öyle de: Maânî-i mukaddesenin, suret-i mülevveseye mücavereti zarar etmez.

For example, you are reflecting on some Divine signs when suddenly you feel ill, or an appetite, or a stimulation like a need to pass water.

Meselâ sen âyât-ı İlahiyeyi tefekkür ediyorsun. Birden bir maraz, ya bir iştiha, ya bevl gibi bir emr-i müheyyic şiddetle senin hissine dokunuyor.

Of course your imagination will see whatever is necessary to cure the ill or answer the need, and will look at it, weave lowly forms appropriate to them, and the meanings that arise will pass between them.

Elbette senin hayalin, deva-i illet ve kaza-i hacetin levazımatını görecek, bakacak, onlara münasib süflî suretleri nescedecek ve gelen manalar ortalarından geçecekler.

But there is no harm in their passing, nor soiling, nor error, nor injury. If there is any mistake, it is in paying them attention and imagining the harm.

Geçeceklere ne beis vardır, ne televvüs var ve ne zarar var ve ne hatar var. Yalnız hatar ise hasr-ı nazardır, zann-ı zarardır.

THIRD ASPECT

Üçüncü Vecih

It is this: there are certain hidden connections between things. There are even the threads of connections between things you least expected.

budur ki: Eşya mabeynlerinde, bazı münasebat-ı hafiye bulunur. Hattâ hiç ümid etmediğin şeyler içinde münasebet ipleri bulunur.

They are either there in fact, or your imagination made them according to the art with which it was preoccupied, and tied them together.

Ya bizzât bulunur veya senin hayalin, meşgul olduğu san'ata göre o ipleri yapmış, onları birbiriyle bağlamış.

It is due to this mystery of connections that sometimes seeing a sacred thing calls to mind a dirty thing.

Şu sırr-ı münasebettendir ki, bazan bir mukaddes şeyi görmek, bir mülevves şeyi hatıra getirir.

As stated in the science of rhetoric, "Although opposition is the cause of distance in the outer world, it is the cause of proximity in the imagination."

Fenn-i Beyan'da beyan olunduğu gibi, "Hariçte uzaklık sebebi olan zıddiyet ise, hayalde sebeb-i kurbiyettir."

That is, an imaginary connection is the means of bringing together the images of two opposites.

Yani: İki zıddın suretlerinin cem'ine vasıta, bir münasebet-i hayaliyedir.

#283

The recollection which arises from this connection is called the association of ideas.

Bu münasebetle gelen tahattura, tedai-yi efkâr tabir edilir.

For example, while performing the prayers or reciting supplications before the Ka'ba in the Divine Presence, this association of ideas takes hold of you and drives you to the furthest, lowest trivia, although you are reflecting on Qur'anic verses.

Meselâ: Sen namazda, münacatta, Kâ'be karşısında, huzur-u İlahîde iken, âyâtı tefekkürde olduğun bir halde; şu tedai-yi efkâr, seni tutup en uzak malayaniyat-ı rezileye sevkeder.

If your head is afflicted with association of ideas in this way, beware, do not be alarmed.

Senin başın, böyle bir tedai-yi efkâra mübtela ise, sakın telaş etme.

Rather, the moment you come to your senses, turn back. Do not say: "I've done a great wrong," and keep playing with the trigger,

Belki intibaha geldiğin anda, dön. "Aman ne kusur ettim" deyip tedkikle meşgul olup durma.

lest through your attention, that tenuous connection strengthens. For the more you feel regret, the more importance you give it and that faint memory of yours becomes ingrained.

Tâ o zaîf münasebet, senin dikkatinle kuvvet peyda etmesin. Zira teessür gösterdikçe, ehemmiyet verdikçe, senin o zaîf tahatturun melekeye döner.

It becomes an imaginary sickness. Do not be frightened, it is not a sickness of the heart.

Bir maraz-ı hayalî olur. Korkma, maraz-ı kalbî değil.

This sort of recollection is mostly involuntary.

Şu nevi tahattur ise, galiben ihtiyarsızdır.

Especially in sensitive, nervous people it is more common. Satan works the mine of this sort of scruple a great deal. The cure for this wound is as follows:

Hususan hassas asabîlerde daha galibdir.

Şeytan, şu nevi vesvesenin madenini çok işlettirir. Şu yaranın merhemi şudur ki:

The association of ideas is mostly involuntary.

Tedai-yi efkâr, galiben ihtiyarsızdır.

One is not answerable for it. In association there is proximity; there is no touching or intermingling.

Onda mes'uliyet yoktur. Hem tedaide, mücaveret var; temas ve ihtilat yoktur.

Therefore the nature of the ideas do not pass to one another and do not harm one another.

Onun için, efkârın keyfiyetleri, birbirine sirayet etmez, birbirine zarar vermez.

Just as Satan and the angel of inspiration being in proximity to one another around the heart, and sinners and the pious being close to one another in the same house cause no harm,

Nasılki şeytan ile melek-i ilham, kalb taraflarında mücaveretleri var ve füccar ve ebrarın karabetleri ve bir meskende durmaları, zarar vermez.

so too, if at the prompting of the association of ideas, dirty imaginings come and enter among clean thoughts, they cause no harm.

Öyle de, tedai-yi efkâr saikasıyla istemediğin pis hayalat, gelip nezih efkârın içine girse; zarar vermez.

Unless it is intentional, or by imagining them to be harmful, one is over-occupied with them.

Meğer kasden olsa veya zarar zannıyla onunla ziyade meşgul olsa.

And sometimes the heart becomes tired, and the mind occupies itself with anything it encounters in order to entertain itself.

Hem bazan kalb yoruluyor. Fikir, kendini eğlendirmek için rastgele bir şeyle meşgul olur.

Then Satan finds an opportunity, and scatters dirty things before it, and eggs it on.

Şeytan fırsat bulur, pis şeyleri önüne serpiyor, sürüyor.

FOURTH ASPECT

Dördüncü Vecih:

This is a scruple arising from searching for the best form of an action. Supposing it to be fear of God, the more rigorous it becomes, the more severe the condition becomes for the person.

Amelin en iyi suretini taharriden neş'et eden bir vesvesedir ki, takva zannıyla teşeddüd ettikçe hal ona şiddetlenir.

It even reaches the point that while searching for even better forms of action, he deviates into what is unlawful.

Hattâ bir dereceye varır ki, o adam amelin daha evlâsını ararken, harama düşer.

Sometimes searching for a Sunna makes him give up what is obligatory.

Bazan bir sünnetin araması, bir vâcibi terkettiriyor.

He says: "I wonder if my act was sound?", and repeats it. This state continues, and he falls into terrible despair.

"Acaba amelim sahih oldu mu?" der, iade eder. Bu hal devam eder. Gayet ye'se düşer.

Satan takes advantage of this state of his, and wounds him. There are two cures for such a wound.

Şeytan şu halinden istifade eder, onu yaralar. Şu yaranın iki merhemi var:

The First Cure: Scruples like this are worthy of the Mu'tazilites, because they say: "Actions and things for which a person is responsible are either, of themselves and in regard to the hereafter, good, and because of this good they were commanded, or they are bad, and because they are bad they were prohibited.

Birinci merhem: Bu gibi vesvese ehl-i İtizale lâyıktır. Çünki onlar derler: "Medar-ı teklif olan ef'al ve eşya, kendi zâtında, âhiret itibariyle ya hüsnü var; sonra o hüsne binaen emredilmiş veya kubhu var; sonra ona binaen nehyedilmiş.

#284

That means, from the point of view of reality and the hereafter, the good and bad in things is dependent on the things themselves, and the Divine command and prohibition follows this."

Demek eşyada, âhiret ve hakikat nokta-i nazarında olan hüsün ve kubh zâtîdir;

emir ve nehy-i İlahî ona tâbi'dir."

According to this school of thought, the following scruple arises in every action which a person performs:

Bu mezhebe göre, insan her işlediği amelde şöyle bir vesvese gelir:

"I wonder if my action was performed in the good way that in essence it is?"

"Acaba amelim nefs-ül emirdeki güzel surette yapılmış mıdır?"

While the true school, the Sunni School, says: "Almighty God orders a thing, then it becomes good.

He prohibits a thing, then it becomes bad."

Amma mezheb-i hak olan Ehl-i Sünnet ve Cemaat derler ki: Cenab-ı Hak bir şeye emreder, sonra hasen olur. Nehyeder, sonra kabih olur.

That is, goodness becomes existent through command, and badness through prohibition.

Demek emir ile güzellik, nehy ile çirkinlik tahakkuk eder.

They look to the awareness of the one who performs the action, and are established according to that.

Hüsün ve kubh mükellefin ıttılaına bakar ve ona göre takarrür eder.

And this good and bad is not in the apparent face which looks to this world, but in the face that looks to the hereafter.

Şu hüsün ve kubh ise, surî ve dünyaya bakan yüzünde değil, belki âhirete bakan yüzdedir.

For example, you performed the prayers or took the ablutions and there was a cause that of itself would spoil them,

Meselâ, sen namaz kıldın veya abdest aldın. Halbuki namazını ve abdestini fesada verecek bir sebeb, nefs-ül emirde varmış.

but you were completely unaware of it. Your prayers and ablutions, therefore, are both sound and acceptable.

Lâkin sen ona hiç muttali olmadın. Senin namazın ve abdestin hem sahihtir, hem hasendir.

However, the Mu'tazilites say: "In reality it was bad and unsound. But it may be accepted from you because you were ignorant and did not know, so you have an excuse."

Mu'tezile der: "Hakikatte kabih ve fasiddir. Lâkin senden kabul edilir. Çünki cehlin var, bilmedin ve özrün var."

Therefore, according to the Sunni School, do not say about an action which is conformable with the externals of the Shari'a: "I wonder if it was sound?"; do not have scruples about it.

Öyle ise Ehl-i Sünnet mezhebine göre, zahir-i şeriate muvafık olarak işlediğin ameline: "Acaba sahih olmuş mu?" deyip vesvese etme.

Say: "Was it accepted?"; do not become proud and conceited!

Fakat, "Kabul olmuş mu?" de. Gururlanma, ucbe girme.

The Second Cure:

This is: There is no difficulty in religion.

{8 Bukhari, i, 16; Ibn Hibban, Sahih, i, 280; Kanz al-‘Ummal, iii, 33, 36, 47; vi, 42, 47.}

İkinci merhem: Dinde harec yoktur.

لاَ حَرَجَ فِى الدِّينِ

Since the four schools of law are true;

Madem dört mezheb haktır.

and since realizing a fault which leads to the seeking of forgiveness is preferable -for the person afflicted with scruples- to seeing actions as good, which leads to pride,

Madem istiğfara müncer olan derk-i kusur ise, gurura müncer olan hüsn-ü amelin rü'yetine -böyle vesveseli adama- müreccahtır.

that is, it is better if such a person sees his action as faulty and seeks forgiveness, rather than considering it to be good and falling into pride;

Yani böyle vesveseli adam, amelini güzel görüp gurura düşmektense, amelini kusurlu görse, istiğfar etse, daha evlâdır.

since it is thus, throw away your scruples and say to Satan: "This state is a difficulty.

Madem böyledir, sen vesveseyi at. Şeytana de ki: Şu hal, bir harecdir.

It is difficult to be aware of the reality of things.

Hakikat-ı hale muttali olmak güçtür.

It is contrary to the ease in religion expressed by: There is no difficulty in religion. It is contrary to the principle, Religion is facility.

Dindeki yüsre münafîdir.

اَلدِّينُ يُسْرٌ لاَ حَرَجَ فِى الدِّينِ

esasına muhaliftir.

Certainly such an action is conformable with a true school of law. That is enough for me.

Elbette böyle amelim bir mezheb-i hakka muvafık gelir. O bana kâfidir.

And at least by admitting my inability to perform the worship in a way worthy of it,

Hem lâakal ben aczimi itiraf ederek ibadeti lâyık-ı vechile eda edemediğimden

it is a means of taking refuge with Divine compassion through humbly beseeching forgiveness, and to meekly supplicating that my faulty actions be accepted.

istiğfar ve tazarru' ile merhamet-i İlahiyeye dehalet edip, kusurum affolunmak, kusurlu amelim kabul olunmak için mütezellilane bir niyaza vesiledir.

FIFTH ASPECT

BEŞİNCİ VECİH:

In matters of belief, what occurs to one in the form of doubts are scruples. The unhappy man suffering from scruples sometimes confuses conceptions in his mind with imaginings.

Mesail-i imaniyede şübhe suretinde gelen vesvesedir. Bîçare vesveseli adam, bazan tahayyülü, taakkul ile iltibas eder.

That is, he imagines a doubt that has occurred to his imagination to be a doubt that has entered his mind, and supposes that his beliefs have been damaged.

Yani: Hayale gelen bir şübheyi, akla girmiş bir şübhe tevehhüm edip, itikadına halel gelmiş zanneder.

#285

Sometimes he supposes a doubt he has imagined to have harmed his belief.

Hem bazan tevehhüm ettiği bir şübheyi, imana zarar veren bir şek zanneder.

Sometimes he supposes a doubt he has imagined to have been confirmed by his reason.

Hem bazan tasavvur ettiği bir şübheyi, tasdik-ı aklîye girmiş bir şübhe zanneder.

Sometimes he supposes pondering over a matter related to unbelief to be unbelief.

Hem bazan bir emr-i küfrîde tefekkürü, küfür zanneder.

That is, he supposes to be contrary to belief his exercising his ability to reflect in the form of understanding the causes of misguidance, and his ability to study and reason in impartial fashion.

Yani dalaletin esbabını anlamak suretinde kuvve-i müfekkirenin cevelanını ve tedkikatını ve bîtarafane muhakemesini, hilaf-ı iman zanneder.

Then, taking fright at these suppositions, which result from the whisperings of Satan, he exclaims: "Alas! My heart is corrupted and my beliefs spoiled."

İşte telkinat-ı şeytaniyenin eseri olan şu zanlardan ürkerek, "Eyvah! Kalbim bozulmuş, itikadıma halel gelmiş" der.

Since those states are mostly involuntary, and he cannot put them to rights through his faculty of will, he falls into despair. The cure for this wound is as follows:

O haller, galiben ihtiyarsız olduğundan, cüz'-i ihtiyarîsiyle ıslah edemediğinden ye'se düşer. Bu yaranın merhemi şudur ki:

Just as imagining unbelief is not unbelief, neither is fancying unbelief, unbelief.

tahayyül-ü küfür, küfür olmadığı gibi; tevehhüm-ü küfür dahi, küfür değildir.

And just as imagining misguidance is not misguidance, so too reflecting on misguidance is not misguidance.

Tasavvur-u dalalet dalalet olmadığı gibi; tefekkür-ü dalalet dahi, dalalet değildir.

For both imagining, and fancying, and supposing, and reflecting, are different from confirmation with the reason and submission of the heart, they are other than them; they are free to an extent;

Çünki hem tahayyül, hem tevehhüm, hem tasavvur, hem tefekkür; tasdik-ı aklîden ve iz'an-ı kalbîden ayrıdırlar, başkadırlar. Onlar bir derece serbesttirler.

they do not listen to the faculty of will; they are not included among the obligations of religion.

Cüz'-i ihtiyariyeyi pek dinlemiyorlar. Teklif-i dinî altına çok giremiyorlar.

But affirmation and submission are not like that; they are dependent on a balance.

Tasdik ve iz'an, öyle değiller. Bir mizana tâbi'dirler.

And just as imagining, fancying, supposing, and reflecting are not affirmation or submission, so they cannot be said to be doubt or hesitation.

Hem tahayyül, tevehhüm, tasavvur, tefekkür, nasılki tasdik ve iz'an değiller. Öyle de şübhe ve tereddüd sayılmazlar.

But if they are repeated unnecessarily and become established,

then a sort of real doubt may be born of them.

Fakat eğer lüzumsuz tekrar ede ede müstakar bir hale gelse,

o vakit hakikî bir nevi şübhe, ondan tevellüd edebilir.

Also, continually taking the part of the opposing side calling it unbiased reasoning or being fair reaches the point that the person involuntarily favours the opposing side.

Hem bîtarafane muhakeme namıyla veya insaf namına deyip, şıkk-ı muhalifi iltizam ede ede, tâ öyle bir hale gelir ki, ihtiyarsız taraf-ı muhalifi iltizam eder.

His taking the part of the truth, which is incumbent on him, is destroyed. He too falls into danger.

Ona vâcib olan hakkın iltizamı kırılır.

O da tehlikeye düşer.

A state of mind becomes fixed in his head whereby he becomes an officious representative of Satan or the enemy.

Hasmın veya şeytanın bir vekil-i fuzulîsi olacak bir halet, zihninde takarrür eder.

The most important of this sort of scruple is this: the person suffering from it confuses something that is actually possible with something which is reasonably possible.

Şu nevi vesvesenin en mühimi budur ki: Vesveseli adam, imkân-ı zâtî ile imkân-ı zihnîyi birbiriyle iltibas eder.

That is, if he sees something which is of itself possible, he imagines it to be reasonably possible and reasonably doubtful.

Yani: Bir şeyi zâtında mümkün görse, o şeyi zihnen dahi mümkün ve aklen meşkuk tevehhüm eder.

Whereas one of the principles of theology (kalâm) is that something which is of itself possible is not opposed to certain knowledge and does not contradict the demands of reason.

Halbuki İlm-i Kelâm'ın kaidelerindendir ki: İmkân-ı zâtî ise, yakîn-i ilmîye münafî değil ve zaruret-i zihniyeye zıddiyeti yoktur.

For example, the Black Sea sinking into the earth at this moment is of itself possible,

(Haşiye-8): Şecere-i hilkatin meyvesi olan insana ve kendi ağacının proğramını ve fihristesini taşıyan meyveye işarettir. Zira kalem-i kudret, âlemin kitab-ı kebirinde ne yazmış ise, icmâlini mahiyet-i insâniyede yazmıştır. Kalem-i kader, dağ gibi bir ağaçta ne yazmış ise, tırnak gibi meyvesinde dahi dercetmiştir.

That means that it is as wonderful to make these inscriptions as to make the whole country. In which case, all the inscriptions, all the works of art, are proclamations of that hidden one, and seals of his.

Demek bu nakışları yapmak, bütün memleketi yapmak kadar hârikadır. Öyle ise herbir nakış, herbir san'at, o gizli zâtın bir ilânnamesidir, bir hâtemidir.
