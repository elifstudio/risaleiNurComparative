#231

Two Tables

Levhalar

[About twenty-five years ago on Yuşa Tepesi (Mount Joshua) above the Istanbul Bosphorus, at a time I had decided to give up the world,

[Bundan yirmibeş sene kadar evvel İstanbul Boğazındaki Yûşa Tepesinde, dünyanın terkine karar verdiğim bir zamanda,

a number of important friends came to me in order to call me back to the world and my former position.

bir kısım mühim dostlarım beni dünyaya, eski vaziyetime döndürmek için yanıma geldiler.

I told them to leave me till the following morning so that I could seek guidance.

Dedim: "Yarına kadar beni bırakınız, istihare edeyim."

That morning the following two Tables were imparted to my heart. They resemble poetry, but they are not.

Sabahleyin kalbime bu iki levha hutur etti. Şiire benzer, fakat şiir değiller.

I have not changed them for the sake of that blessed memory, and they have been kept as they occurred to me.

O mübarek hatıranın hatırı için ilişmedim. Geldiği gibi muhafaza edildi.

They were added to the Twenty-Third Word, and now have been included here on account of their ‘station’.]

Yirmiüçüncü Söz'ün âhirine ilhak edilmişti. Makam münasebetiyle buraya alındı.]

The First Table

Birinci Levha

[The Table depicting the reality of the world of the heedless.]

[Ehl-i gaflet dünyasının hakikatını tasvir eder levhadır.]

Don’t call me to the world;
I came, and saw it was transitory.

Beni dünyaya çağırma
Ona geldim fena gördüm.

Heedlessness was a veil;
I saw the light of truth was concealed.

Dema gaflet hicab oldu
Ve nur-u Hak nihan gördüm.

All the beings in existence,
I saw were ephemeral, harmful.

Bütün eşya-yı mevcûdât
Birer fâni muzır gördüm.

If you say, being, I dressed in it;
Alas! It was non-being; I suffered much!

Vücud desen onu giydim
Ah ademdi çok bela gördüm.

If you say, life, I tasted it;
I saw it was torment upon torment.

Hayat desen onu tattım
Azab ender azab gördüm.

The mind became pure torture;
I saw permanence to be tribulation.

Akıl ayn-ı ikab oldu
Bekayı bir bela gördüm.

Life became pure whim;
I saw attainment to be pure loss.

Ömür ayn-ı heva oldu
Kemâl ayn-ı heba gördüm.

Deeds became pure hypocrisy;
I saw hopes to be pure pain.

Amel ayn-ı riyâ oldu
Emel ayn-ı elem gördüm.

Union became parting itself;
I saw the cure to be the ill.

Visâl, nefs-i zeval oldu
Devayı ayn-ı dâ' gördüm.

These lights became darkness;
I saw these friends to be orphans.

Bu envar, zulümat oldu
Bu ahbabı yetim gördüm.

These voices became announcements of death;
I saw the living to be dead.

Bu savtlar, na'y-ı mevt oldu
Bu ahyayı mevat gördüm.

Knowledge was transformed into fancy;
I saw in science a thousand ailments.

Ulûm, evhama kalboldu
Hikemde bin sekam gördüm.

Pleasure became pure pain;
I saw existence to be compounded non-existence.

Lezzet, ayn-ı elem oldu
Vücudda bin adem gördüm.

If you say the Beloved, I found him;
Alas! On separation I suffered grievous pain.

Habib desen onu buldum
Ah! Firakta çok elem gördüm.

#232

The Second Table

İkinci Levha

[The Table indicating the reality of the world of the people of guidance, those with easy hearts.]

[Ehl-i hidâyet ve huzurun hakikat-ı dünyalarına işaret eder levhadır.]

Then the heedlessness passed,
And I saw the light of truth clearly.

Dema gaflet zeval buldu
Ve nur-u Hak ayan gördüm.

Existence became the proof of God;
See, life is the mirror of God.

Vücud, bürhân-ı Zât oldu
Hayat, mir'at-ı Hak'tır gör.

The mind became the key to treasuries.
See, transience is the door to permanence.

Akıl, miftah-ı kenz oldu
Fena, bab-ı bekâdır gör.

The spark of perfection died,
But, behold the Sun of Beauty!

Kemâlin lem'ası söndü
Fakat, şems-i Cemâl var gör.

Separation became true union;
See, pain is pure pleasure.

Zeval, ayn-ı visal oldu
Elem, ayn-ı lezzettir gör.

Life became pure action;
See, eternity is pure life.

Ömür nefs-i amel oldu
Ebed ayn-ı ömürdür gör.

Darkness became the container of light;
See, there is true life in death.

Zalâm zarf-ı ziya oldu
Bu mevtte hak hayat var gör.

All things became familiar;
See, all sounds are the mentioning of God.

Bütün eşya enîs oldu
Bütün asvat zikirdir gör.

All the atoms in existence –
See, each recites God’s praises and extols Him.

Bütün zerrat-ı mevcûdât
Birer zâkir, müsebbih gör.

I found poverty to be a treasury of wealth;
See, in impotence is perfect strength.

Fakrı kenz-i gına buldum
Aczde tam kuvvet var gör.

If you find God,
See, all things are yours.

Eğer Allah'ı buldunsa
Bütün eşya senindir gör.

If you are the slave of the Owner of All Things,
See, His property is yours.

Eğer Mâlik-i Mülk'e memlûk isen
Onun mülkü senindir gör.

If you are arrogant and claim to own yourself,
See, it is trial and tribulation without end;
Experience its boundless torment;
See, it is a calamity most crushing.

Eğer hodbin ve kendi nefsine mâlik isen:
Bilâ-addin beladır gör,
Belâ-haddin azabdır tad,
Belâ-gâyet ağırdır gör.

If you are a true slave of God,
See, it is a limitless pleasure and ease.
Taste its uncountable rewards,
Experience its infinite happiness...

Eğer hakikî abd-i hudabin isen
Hududsuz bir sâfadır gör.
Hesabsız bir sevab var tad
Nihayetsiz saadet gör...

#233

He is the Enduring One

Huvel Baki

[Twenty-five years ago in Ramadan after the Afternoon Prayer, I read Shaykh
‘Abd al-Qadir Geylani’s composition in verse about the Most Beautiful Names.

[Yirmibeş sene evvel Ramazanda ikindiden sonra Şeyh-i Geylanî'nin (K.S.) Esmâ-i Hüsnâ manzumesini okudum.

I felt a desire to write a supplication with the Divine Names, and at that time only this much was written.

Bana bir arzu geldi ki: Esmâ-i hüsnâ ile bir münacat yazayım. Fakat o vakit bu kadar yazıldı.

I wanted to write a supplication similar to that of my holy master, but, alas! I have no ability to write poetry and it remained deficient.

O kudsî üstadımın mübarek Münâcât-ı Esmâiyesine bir nazîre yapmak istedim. Heyhat!.. Nazma istidadım yok. Yapamadım, noksan kaldı.

Nevertheless, the supplication was added to the Thirty-Third Letter of the Thirty-Third Word, known as the Thirty-Three Windows, then was included here on account of its ‘station’.]

Bu münacat, Otuzüçüncü Söz'ün Otuzüçüncü Mektub'u olan Pencereler Risalesine ilhak edilmişti. Makam münasebetiyle buraya alındı.]

He is the Enduring One
The Wise Judge of affairs, we are under His decree;
He is the Just Arbiter; His are the heavens and the earth.
The One Knowing of the secrets and hidden matters in His dominions;
He is the All-Powerful, Self-Subsistent; His is all from the Throne to the ground.
The Perceiver of the fine points and embroideries in His art;
He is the Creator, the Loving One; His is the beauty and the splendour.
The Glorious One Whose attributes are reflected in the mirrors in His creation; He is the Lord, the Most Holy; His is the might and the grandeur.
The Originator of creatures; we form the embroideries of His art;
He is the Constant, the Enduring One; His is the dominion and eternity.
The Munificent Bestower of gifts; we are the caravan of His guests; He is the Provider, the Sufficer; His is the praise and laudation.
The Beauteous Granter of gifts; We are the weavings of His knowledge; He is the Creator, the Faithful; His is the munificence, the giving.
The Hearer of plaints and supplications in His creation;
He is the Merciful, the Healer; His are the thanks and the praise.
The Pardoner of the faults and sins of His servants;
He is the Oft Forgiving, the Compassionate; His is forgiveness and acceptance.

هُوَ الْبَاقِى
حَكِيمُ الْقَضَايَا نَحْنُ فِى قَبْضِ حُكْمِهِ ٭ هُوَ الْحَكَمُ الْعَدْلُ لَهُ اْلاَرْضُ وَ السَّمَاءُ
عَلِيمُ الْخَفَايَا وَ الْغُيُوبِ فِى مُلْكِهِ ٭ هُوَ الْقَادِرُ الْقَيُّومُ لَهُ الْعَرْشُ وَ الثَّرَاءُ
لَطِيفُ الْمَزَايَا وَ النُّقُوشِ فِى صُنْعِهِ ٭ هُوَ الْفَاطِرُ الْوَدُودُ لَهُ الْحُسْنُ وَ الْبَهَاءُ
جَلِيلُ الْمَرَايَا وَ الشُّؤُنِ فِى خَلْقِهِ ٭ هُوَ الْمَلِكُ الْقُدُّوسُ لَهُ الْعِزُّ وَ الْكِبْرِيَاءُ
بَدِيعُ الْبَرَايَا نَحْنُ مِنْ نَقْشِ صُنْعِهِ ٭ هُوَ الدَّائِمُ الْبَاقِى لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَ الْبَقَاءُ
كَرِيمُ الْعَطَايَا نَحْنُ مِنْ رَكْبِ ضَيْفِهِ ٭ هُوَ الرَّزَّاقُ الْكَافِى لَهُ الْحَمْدُ وَ الثَّنَاءُ
جَمِيلُ الْهَدَايَا نَحْنُ مِنْ نَسْجِ عِلْمِهِ ٭ هُوَ الْخَالِقُ الْوَافِى لَهُ الْجُودُ وَ الْعَطَاءُ
سَمِيعُ الشَّكَايَا وَ الدُّعَاءِ لِخَلْقِهِ ٭ هُوَ الرَّاحِمُ الشَّافِى لَهُ الشُّكْرُ وَ الثَّنَاءُ
غَفُورُ الْخَطَايَا وَ الذُّنُوبِ لِعَبْدِهِ ٭ هُوَ الْغَفَّارُ الرَّحِيمُ لَهُ الْعَفْوُ وَ الرِّضَاءُ

O my soul! Together with my heart, weep and cry, and say:

Ey nefsim! Kalbim gibi ağla ve bağır ve de ki:

I am ephemeral; I do not want one such as that.
I am impotent; I do not want one such as that.

"Fâniyim, fâni olanı istemem.
Âcizim, âciz olanı istemem.

I have surrendered my spirit to the Most Merciful; I do not want another.

Ruhumu Rahman'a teslim eyledim, gayr istemem.

I want one, but I want an eternal friend.
I am a mere speck, but I want an eternal sun.

İsterim, fakat bir yâr-ı bâki isterim.
Zerreyim, fakat bir şems-i sermed isterim.

I am nothing, but nothing, yet I want these beings, all of them.

Hiç ender hiçim, fakat bu mevcûdâtı umumen isterim."

#234

A Fruit Of The Pine, Cedar, Juniper, And Black Cypress Trees In The Uplands Of Barla

Barla Yaylası; Çam, Katran, Ardıç, Karakavağın Bir Meyvesidir

[While being a part of the Eleventh Letter, this has been included here on account of its 'stations'.]

[Makam münasebetiyle buraya alınmış. Onbirinci Mektub'un bir parçasıdır.]

One time during my captivity while gazing at the majestic and wonderful forms of the pine, cedar, and juniper trees on the mountain top, a gentle breeze was blowing.

Bir vakit esaretimde dağ başında âzametli çam ve katran ve ardıç ağaçlarının heybet-nümâ sûretlerini, hayret-fezâ vaziyetlerini temaşa ederken pek lâtif bir rüzgâr esti.

Transforming the scene into a magnificent, delightful, and clamorous display of dancing and a rapturous performance of praise and glorification,

O vaziyeti, pek muhteşem ve şirin velvele-âlûd bir zelzele-i raks-nümâ, bir tesbihât-ı cezbe-edâ sûretine çevirdiğinden;

the enjoyment of watching it was transformed into instruction for my eyes and wisdom for my ears.

eğlence temaşası, nazar-ı ibrete ve sem'-i hikmete döndü.

I suddenly recalled the Kurdish lines of Ahmad al-Jizri, whose meaning is:

Birden Ahmed-i Cizrî'nin kürdçe şu fıkrası:

‘Everyone has hastened to gaze at You and Your Beauty; they are acting coyly
before Your Beauty.’

My heart wept as follows, expressing their instructive meanings.

Thus the trees show themselves to be not lifeless, unconscious bodies, but highly conscious and meaningful.

o ağaçlar câmid, şuursuz cisim gibi değil.. belki gâyet şuurkârane mânidar vaziyetleri gösteriyorlar.

[One time at night in a high spot on Çamdağı (the Pine Mountain) near Barla I was looking at the face of the heavens when the following lines suddenly occurred to me.

[Bir vakit Barla'da Çam Dağı'nda yüksek bir mevkide, gecede semânın yüzüne baktım. Gelecek fıkralar, birden hutûr etti.

The Utterance Of The Stars

Yıldızları Konuşturan Bir Yıldızname

Then listen to the stars, listen to their harmonious address!
See what wisdom has emblazed on the decree of its light!

Dinle de yıldızları şu hutbe-i şîrînine
Name-i nurîn-i hikmet, bak ne takrir eylemiş.

Altogether they start to speak with the tongue of truth,
They address the majesty of the All-Powerful One of Glory’s sovereignty:

Hep beraber nutka gelmiş, hak lisanıyla derler;
"Bir Kadîr-i Zülcelâl'in haşmet-i Sultanına

We are each of us light-scattering proofs of the existence of our Maker;
We are witnesses both to His Unity and His Power;

Birer bürhân-ı nur-efşanız vücûd-u Sâni'a
Hem vahdete, hem kudrete şâhidleriz biz.

We are subtle miracles gilding the face of the skies
For the angels to make excursions on;

Şu zeminin yüzünü yaldızlayan
Nâzenin mu'cizâtı çün melek seyranına.

We are the innumerable attentive eyes of the heavens
That watch the earth, that study Paradise;*

Bu semânın arza bakan, cennete dikkat eden
Binler müdakkik gözleriz biz (Haşiye)

*{That is, since innumerable miracles of power are exhibited on the face of the earth, which is the seedbed and tillage for Paradise,

{(Haşiye): Yâni Cennet çiçeklerinin fidanlık ve mezraacığı olan zeminin yüzünde hadsiz mu'cizât-ı kudret teşhir edildiğinden;

the angels in the world of the heavens gaze on those miracles, those marvels.

Semâvat âlemindeki melâikeler o mu'cizâtı, o hârikaları temaşa ettikleri gibi,

And like the angels, the stars, which are like the eyes of the heavenly bodies, gaze on the finely fashioned creatures on the earth, and in so doing look at the world of Paradise.

ecram-ı semâviyenin gözleri hükmünde olan yıldızlar dahi, güya melâikeler gibi zemin yüzündeki nâzenin masnuatı gördükçe Cennet âlemine bakıyorlar.

At the same time they look on both the earth and Paradise; they observe those fleeting wonders in an enduring form in Paradise.

O muvakkat hârikaları bâki bir sûrette Cennet'te dahi müşahede ediyorlar gibi bir zemine, bir Cennet'e bakıyorlar.

That is to say, there, there are prospects of both worlds.}

Yâni o iki âleme nezaretleri var demektir.}

We are the innumerable exquisite fruits that the hand of wisdom of the Beauteous One of Glory has fastened
To the celestial portion of the tree of creation, to all the branches of the Milky Way;

Tûbâ-i hilkatten semâvat şıkkına, hep Kehkeşan ağsanına
Bir Cemil-i Zülcelâl'in, dest-i hikmetle takılmış pek güzel meyveleriyiz biz.

For the inhabitants of the heavens, we are each of us a travelling mosque, a spinning house, a lofty home,
Each is an illumining lamp, a mighty ship, an aeroplane;

Şu semâvat ehline birer mescid-i seyyar, birer hâne-i devvar, birer ulvî âşiyâne,
Birer misbah-ı nevvar, birer gemi-i cebbar, birer tayyareleriz biz.

We are each of us a miracle of power of the All-Powerful One of Perfection, The All- Wise One of Glory;
Each a wonder of His creative art, a rarity of His wisdom, a marvel of His creation, a world of light;

Bir Kadîr-i Zülkemâl'in, bir Hakîm-i Zülcelâl'in birer mu'cize-i kudret
Birer hârika-i san'at-ı Hâlıkane; birer nâdire-i hikmet, birer dâhiye-i hilkat, birer nur âlemiyiz biz.

We demonstrated to mankind innumerable proofs, we made them hear with these innumerable tongues of ours;
But their accursed unseeing, unbelieving eyes did not see our faces, they did not hear our words; and we are signs that speak the truth;

Böyle yüzbin dil ile yüzbin bürhân gösteririz, işittiririz insan olan insana.
Kör olası dinsiz gözü, görmez oldu yüzümüzü, hem işitmez sözümüzü, hak söyleyen âyetleriz biz.

Our stamp is one; our seal is one; we are mastered by our Sustainer; We glorify Him through our subjugation; We recite His Names;
We are each of us in ecstasy, a member of the mighty circle of the Milky Way.

Sikkemiz bir, turramız bir, Rabbimize müsahharız. Müsebbihiz, zikrederiz abîdane.
Kehkeşanın halka-i kübrâsına mensub birer meczublarız biz." dediklerini hayalen dinledim.

The Eighteenth Word

Onsekizinci Söz

[This Word consists of two Stations, of which the Second has not yet been written. There are three Points in the First Station.]

[Bu sözün iki makamı var. İkinci Makamı daha yazılmamıştır. Birinci Makamı üç noktadır.]

First Point

Birinci Nokta

In the Name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

Think not that those who exult in what they have thus contrived, and who love to be praised for what they have not done – think not that they will escape suffering: for grievous suffering does await them.{Qur’an, 3:188.}

لاَ َتحْسَبَنَّ الَّذِينَ يَفْرَحُونَ ِبمَآ اَتَوْا وَيُحِبُّونَ اَنْ يُحْمَدُوا ِبمَا لَمْ يَفْعَلُوا فَلاَ َتحْسَبَنَّهُمْ ِبمَفَازَةٍ مِنَ الْعَذَابِ وَلَهُمْ عَذَابٌ اَلِيمٌ

A Chastening Slap for my Evil-Commanding Soul:

Nefs-i emmâreme bir sille-i tedib:

O my foolish soul, charmed at glory, enamoured of fame, addicted to praise, and without equal in egotism!

Ey fahre meftun, şöhrete mübtelâ, medhe düşkün, hodbinlikte bîhemta sersem nefsim!

If it is a just claim that the source of the fig and its thousands of fruits is its tiny seed, and that all the grapes in their hundred bunches hanging from a dry branch of the vine are produced through the skill of the branch, and that those who benefit from them should praise and esteem the branch and the seed,

Eğer binler meyve veren incirin menşei olan küçücük bir çekirdeği ve yüz salkım ona takılan üzümün siyah kurucuk çubuğu; bütün o meyveleri, o salkımları kendi hünerleri olduğu ve onlardan istifade edenler o çubuğa, o çekirdeğe medh ve hürmet etmek lâzım olduğu, hak bir dâva ise;

then perhaps you have the right to be proud and conceited about the bounties with which you are loaded.

senin dahi sana yüklenen nimetler için fahre, gurura belki bir hakkın var.

But in fact you deserve to be constantly chided, because you are not like the seed and the branch:

Halbuki sen, daim zemme müstehaksın. Zira o çekirdek ve o çubuk gibi değilsin.

Since you have the faculty of will, you reduce the value of those bounties through your pride.

Senin bir cüz'-i ihtiyarın bulunmakla, o nimetlerin kıymetlerini fahrin ile tenkîs ediyorsun.

Through your conceit, you destroy them; through your ingratitude, you nullify them; through claiming them as your own, you lay hands on them unlawfully.

Gururunla tahrib ediyorsun ve küfranınla ibtal ediyorsun ve temellükle gasbediyorsun.

Your duty is not to glory in your deeds, but to offer thanks.

Senin vazifen fahr değil, şükürdür.

What is fit for you is not fame, but humility.

Sana lâyık olan şöhret değil, tevazudur, hacâlettir.

Your right is not praise, it is repentance and to seek forgiveness.

Senin hakkın medih değil istiğfardır, nedâmettir.

Your perfection lies not in self-centredness, but in recognizing God.

Senin kemâlin hodbinlik değil, hüdâbinliktedir.

Yes, you in my body resemble ‘nature’ in the world.

Evet sen benim cismimde, âlemdeki tabiata benzersin.

Both of you were created to receive good and be the thing to which evil is referred.

İkiniz, hayrı kabûl etmek, şerre merci olmak için yaratılmışsınız.

That is to say, you are not the agent and source, but the recipient and passive.

Yâni fâil ve masdar değilsiniz, belki münfail ve mahalsiniz.

You have only an effect, and that is being the cause of evil because you did not accept as you should have done a good arising from absolute good.

Yalnız bir tesiriniz var: O da hayr-ı mutlaktan gelen hayrı, güzel bir sûrette kabûl etmemenizden şerre sebeb olmanızdır.

#240

Also you were both created as veils, so that things that are apparently ugly, whose beauty is not obvious, would be attributed to you, and you would be means of the Most Holy Divine Essence being acknowledged free of defect.

Hem siz birer perde yaratılmışsınız. Tâ güzelliği görülmeyen zâhirî çirkinlikler size isnad edilip, Zât-ı Mukaddese-i İlahiye'nin tenzihine vesile olasınız.

But you have taken on a form entirely contrary to the duty of your natures.

Halbuki bütün bütün vazife-i fıtratınıza zıd bir sûret giymişsiniz.

Although out of your incapacity you have transformed good into evil, you as though act as partners to your Creator.

Kabiliyetsizliğinizden hayrı şerre kalbettiğiniz halde, Hâlık'ınızla güya iştirak edersiniz.

That means one who worships himself and worships nature is extremely foolish and perpetrates a great wrong.

Demek nefisperest, tabiatperest gâyet ahmak, gâyet zâlimdir.

And do not say: ‘I am a place of manifestation, and one who manifests beauty becomes beautiful’.

Hem deme ki: "Ben mazharım. Güzele mazhar ise, güzelleşir".

For you have not assimilated it, so are not a place of manifestation but a place of passage.

Zira, temessül etmediğinden mazhar değil, memer olursun.

And do not say: ‘Among people I was chosen. These fruits, these fine works, are shown through me. That means I have some merit.’

Hem deme ki: “Halk içinde ben intihab edildim. Bu meyveler benim ile gösteriliyor. Demek bir meziyetim var.”

No! God forbid! Rather they were given to you first because you are more bankrupt, needy, and sad than everyone else!*

Hâyır, hâşâ! Belki herkesten evvel sana verildi; çünki herkesten ziyade sen müflis ve muhtaç ve müteellim olduğundan en evvel senin eline verildi. (Haşiye)

*{Truly, I was extremely pleased at the New Said silencing his soul to this extent in this dispute, and said, “A thousand bravo’s!”}

{(Haşiye): Hakikaten ben de bu münazarada Yeni Said, nefsini bu derece ilzam ve iskat etmesini çok beğendim ve “Bin Bârekâllah” dedim.}

Second Point

İkinci Nokta

This Point elucidates one meaning of the verse: Who has created everything in the best way,{Qur’an, 32:7} and is as follows:

اَحْسَنَ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ خَلَقَهُ âyetinin bir sırrını izah eder. Şöyle ki:

In everything, even the things which appear to be the most ugly, there is an aspect of true beauty.

Her şeyde, hattâ en çirkin görünen şeylerde, hakikî bir hüsün ciheti vardır.

Yes, everything in the universe, every event, is either in itself beautiful, which is called ‘essential beauty’, or it is beautiful in regard to its results, which is called ‘relative beauty’.

Evet kâinattaki her şey, her hâdise ya bizzât güzeldir, ona hüsn-ü bizzât denilir. Veya neticeleri cihetiyle güzeldir ki, ona hüsn-ü bilgayr denilir.

There are certain events which are apparently ugly and confused, but beneath that apparent veil, there are most shining instances of beauty and order.

Bir kısım hâdiseler var ki, zâhirî çirkin, müşevveştir. Fakat o zâhirî perde altında gâyet parlak güzellikler ve intizâmlar var.

Beneath the veil of stormy rains and muddy soil in the season of spring are hidden the smiles of innumerable beautiful flowers and well-ordered plants.

Ezcümle:Bahar mevsiminde fırtınalı yağmur, çamurlu toprak perdesi altında nihayetsiz güzel çiçek ve muntâzam nebâtatın tebessümleri saklanmış

And behind the veils of the harsh destruction and mournful separations of autumn is the discharge from the duties of their lives of the amiable small animals, the friends of the coy flowers, so as to preserve them from the blows and torments of winter events, which are manifestations of Divine might and glory, and under the veil of which the way is paved for the new and beautiful spring.

ve güz mevsiminin haşin tahribatı, hazîn firak perdeleri arkasında tecelliyat-ı Celaliye-i Sübhaniye'nin mazharı olan kış hâdiselerinin tazyikinden ve tazibinden muhafaza etmek için nazdar çiçeklerin dostları olan nazenin hayvancıkları vazife-i hayattan terhis etmekle beraber, o kış perdesi altında nazenin taze güzel bir bahara yer ihzâr etmektir.

Beneath the veil of events like storms, earthquakes, and plague, is the unfolding of numerous hidden immaterial flowers.

Fırtına, zelzele, veba gibi hâdiselerin perdeleri altında gizlenen pek çok mânevî çiçeklerin inkişafı vardır.

The seeds of many potentialities which have not developed sprout and grow beautiful on account of events which are apparently ugly.

Tohumlar gibi neşv ü nemâsız kalan birçok istidad çekirdekleri, zâhirî çirkin görünen hâdiseler yüzünden sünbüllenip güzelleşir.

As though general upheavals and universal change are all immaterial rain.

Güya umum inkılâblar ve küllî tahavvüller, birer mânevî yağmurdur.
